Expert Response

Project Duration: 6 months

What is Expert Response?

Expert Response is a responsive web app designed to allow users to connect instantly with an expert virtually in any field. The app is designed for those who seek professional advice whether at work, at home, or on the go

My Role

  • UX Researcher
  • UX and UI Designer

The Problem

Our users need a way to connect instantly with an expert in a specific field to help answer questions because they want to be more informed and prepared before they face the task or problem at hand.

Hypothesis Statement

By creating an expert-booking platform, we can enable curious learners to gain convenient access to professional guidance allowing them to be more informed and prepared.

The Design Process

Understand

Seeking advice takes many forms. For some, it may be seeking out a professional. For others, it may be turning to family or friends. The lack of relative research required me to find a way to delve into the issue.

Research

To gain a more comprehensive understanding of my users, I conducted exploratory research using quantitative and qualitative methods.

Analyze

With having validated the problem statement and hypothesis through research, I created user flows, personas, and journeys to visualize and maintain a connection with the users.

From the research collected and empathy built, they will act as an anchor throughout the design process to ensure the users needs are met.

Site Map

With the help of card sorting, I was able to create a sitemap using the mobile-first approach.

Ideate

After creating a visual of the users needs, it was time to start sketching out low-fidelity wireframes of Expert Response. This allowed me to rapidly explore different ideas and layouts.

I thought out and sketched many design layouts that would best fit the users needs and demands. I discovered many designs that would best fit for prototyping.

Prototype

Once the sketching was complete, I moved on to creating mid-fidelity wireframes with Figma. I realized early on that there were many features and factors that I did not previously account for.

Having completed the mid-fidelity wireframes, I developed them into a high-fidelity working prototype that will be ready for testing.

Test

Equipped with a high-fidelity working prototype, I started to devise an evaluative research plan to test my design using both quantitative and qualitative methods.

It was very rewarding seeing and observing Expert Response in action! I was pleased when features acted as planned, but I was very pleased to see features that did not.

Usability Issues and Solutions

One of the scenario tasks was to locate an existing chat with a mechanic. I found that over half of the participants struggled locating the mechanic within their inbox.

1st Iteration

Originally, users would go into their inbox and see their existing chats with the intention that users would know the professions of each expert.

Issue 1

“I don’t know who everyone is that I’ve talked to”

Almost all mentioned something similar when attempting the task. The participants attempted to guess the expert's profession based on their picture.

Before I could add the profession of each expert, I needed to rethink the layout. I went back to sketching low-fidelity wireframes to locate the best design. Once I determined the best design, I incorporated it into my existing high-fidelity prototype.

Solution

I moved the date down and put the profession on the right side of the name in the inbox. For the chat, the profession went underneath the name.

Another issue found was the beginning of the onboarding process.

A few participants skipped the onboarding process, and a couple participants were confused about what the first page was.

Issue 2

This page confused almost half of the participants. They kept asking whether to press the “skip” or “explore” button.

Solution

To fix the confusion, I removed the skip button so users will have to use the “explore” button. However, on the next two pages, the skip button still exists.

Refined Design

In addition to the usability testing, Expert Response went through preference tests, emotional design strategies, peer feedback, accessibility guidelines, and mentor consultations.
